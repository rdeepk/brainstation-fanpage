About Zakir Hussain

Zakir Hussain is today appreciated both in the field of percussion and in the music world at large as an international phenomenon. A classical tabla virtuoso of the highest order, his consistently brilliant and exciting performances have not only established him as a national treasure but gained him worldwide fame.

He's a two-time Grammy winner and a pioneer of world music.

More about his exemplary Music

Zakir Hussain has been playing professionally since in 1963. The musician has accompanied many of India’s greatest classical musicians and dancers, and played and collaborated with musical luminaries such as YoYo Ma, Bela Fleck and George Harrison. In 2011, Hussain began experimenting with fusing Celtic and Indian, and the resulting ensemble successfully toured the U.S. and India. He is now bringing that collaboration back to North America with a group that includes bodhrán player John Joe Kelly, fiddle player Charlie McKerron, bansuri player Rakesh Chaurasia, piper Fraser Fifield, fiddle player Patsy Reid, guitarist Tony Byrne, violinist Ganesh Rajagopalan and flautist Jean-Michel Veillon
